Mountains

Mountains are majestic landforms that rise prominently above their surroundings due to geological processes. They are characterized by their significant elevation, steep slopes, and generally rugged terrain. Mountains cover about 22% of the Earth's land surface and play a crucial role in shaping landscapes, influencing weather patterns, and supporting diverse ecosystems.
